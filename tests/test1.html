If you think of all the things that are required for photosynthesis, can you predict what will be the best conditions for photosynthesis to occur in?

Divide the class into three groups. Each group will investigate what the optimal soil, water and light conditions are for photosynthesis.

What condition for photosynthesis are you investigating:

{Your teacher will prepare enough bean / lentil / basil seedlings for everyone in the class to have their own.}

  • A container to plant your seedling in (it should be able to hold at least 300 ml). This can be margarine or yoghurt tub, a jam or coffee tin or any other similar plastic or metal container

  • rich dark soil to place in the container and plant your seedling in

  • water in a bucket to water all the different plants when necessary

  • You need to poor 60 ml of water on the plants everytime you water any plant. That is two tablespoons. If you know anyone who feeds a baby formula, the spoons they use to measure the formula often holds 30ml. Alternatively you can borrow your mom’s measuring spoons from home. (AND if you’re lucky your teacher might have some spare measuring spoons in her class.)

  • Dark cupboard

  • Paper bags or dark black bags and a 30 cm stick or twig

If you are doing the soil test, you will also need:

  • cotton wool or torn strips of newspaper (about 1 cm wide and 7 - 10 cm long)

  • at least two different types of soil: rich dark soil, river or beach sand or clay soil

Soil Test:

  1. When the seeds have sprouted, remove the top layer of cotton wool.
  2. Choose a type of ‘soil’ to grow your seedling in - either rich dark soil, sand or clay, or you can choose to keep your seedling in newspaper cuttings or cotton wool to see what will happen.
  3. If you are using newspaper cuttings or cotton wool, fill your container with this. If you chose soil, sand or clay, fill your container with that instead.
  4. WRITE THE TYPE OF ‘SOIL’ YOU USED ON YOUR CONTAINER.
  5. Make a little hole with your thumb and plant your seeding into this. Cover the root with soil/newspaper cuttings/cotton wool and let the little stem and primary leaves stay above the ground.
  6. Now place one container with cotton wool, one with rich soil and one with the sand / clay in a sunny spot. Water your seedlings regularly. Make sure the soil is moist but not soaking wet.
  7. Do this for three weeks.

Water Test:

  1. When the seeds have sprouted, remove the top layer of cotton wool.

  2. Place the rich dark soil into your container - don’t fill the container but leave about 2 cm space between the soil and the rim of the container.

  3. Make a little hole with your thumb and plant your seeding into this. Cover the root with soil and let the little stem and primary leaves stay above the ground.

  4. Divide your group into five smaller groups.

    1. The first group must write on their containers: NO WATER

    2. The next group must write on their containers: WATER EVERY 9/10 DAYS

    3. Another group must write on their containers: WATER ONCE A WEEK

    4. A group must write on their containers: WATER EVERY 2 - 3 DAYS (keep the soil moist but not soaking wet).

    5. The last group must write on their containers: WATER EVERYDAY

  5. Make sure to water your plant exactly as your label states.

  6. Do this for three weeks.

Light Test:

  1. When the seeds have sprouted, remove the top layer of cotton wool.

  2. Place the rich dark soil into your container - don’t fill the container but leave about 2 cm space between the soil and the rim of the container.

  3. Make a little hole with your thumb and plant your seeding into this. Cover the root with soil and let the little stem and primary leaves stay above the ground.

  4. Divide your group into four smaller groups.

    1. The first group must place their containers and plants into a DARK cupboard where the plant will not get any light. Write on these containers: NO LIGHT. REMEMBER to water this plant every regularly!

    2. One group must place their plants into a brown paper bag or a dark / black plastic bag and prop it up with a stick or twig to protect the plant. Place your plants in a shady part of the class where the sun cannot bake on it and overheat the bag.

      • At the start of your Natural Science lessons you need to remove the plant and if possible, place it in a sunny spot in the class.

      • At the end of your Natural Science lessons you need to place the plant back its shady part of the class and cover it with the bag again.

      • Write on these containers: LIGHT ONLY DURING SCIENCE CLASS.

    3. Another group must keep their containers as far from the window as possible. Write on these containers: SHADED LIGHT.

    4. The last group must place their plants in a sunny spot very close to the window. Write on these containers: FULL DAYLIGHT

  5. Make sure to water your plants regularly but not to over-water or flood them. The soil should be moist but not soaking wet.

  6. Do this for three weeks.
